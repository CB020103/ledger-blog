This is a fictional 180-unit garden-style community in Jacksonville, FL, and it's the one I go back to whenever I want to sharpen the fundamentals. No cap-rate compression story, no macro trend, just a clean acquisition underwrite: current rent roll, a realistic expense stack, an agency-style acquisition loan, and a five-year hold.

The going-in cap rate sets the purchase price (rather than the other way around), which keeps the model honest: if I flex the exit cap rate or the vacancy assumption, everything downstream recalculates instead of quietly going stale. Same with the loan payoff at exit: instead of building out a full amortization schedule, I solved for the remaining balance directly off the standard formula, which is the kind of shortcut that's obvious in hindsight but easy to miss the first time you build one of these.

The result: a 5.60% going-in cap rate that lands at a 9.6% levered IRR and a 1.55x equity multiple over five years, nothing spectacular, which is the point. It's a base-case, no-story deal, built to make sure the underwriting logic itself holds up before I lean on it for something more interesting.
